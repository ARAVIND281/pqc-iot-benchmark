\subsection{TOPSIS Decision Framework}

Evaluating multi-dimensional parameters spanning Speed, Energy, and Overheads simultaneously creates complex, seemingly irreconcilable comparisons where classical intuitions normally falter. We integrate the formal Technique for Order of Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS) framework \cite{hwang1981multiple} calculating the shortest scalar gradient distancing alternatives respectively from designated ideal structures ($v^+$) while actively maximizing distance isolating explicit anti-ideal matrices ($v^-$).

\begin{algorithm}
\caption{TOPSIS Calculation Routine}\label{alg:topsis}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Decision Matrix $X_{m \times n}$, Weights $W_n$
\State \textbf{Norm:} $R_{ij} \Gets X_{ij} / \sqrt{\sum X_{ij}^2}$
\State \textbf{Weight:} $V_{ij} \Gets R_{ij} \cdot W_j$
\State \textbf{Ideals:} Determine $A^+$ and $A^-$ per criterion
\State $D_i^+ \Gets \sqrt{\sum (V_{ij} - V_j^+)^2}$
\State $D_i^- \Gets \sqrt{\sum (V_{ij} - V_j^-)^2}$
\State $\text{Closeness } C_i \Gets \frac{D_i^-}{D_i^+ + D_i^-}$
\State \Return Rank uniquely descending by scalar $C_i$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
