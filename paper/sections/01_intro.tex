%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The foundational pillars of modern digital security---specifically RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC)---are mathematically fragile against cryptographically relevant quantum computers (CRQCs). Shor's algorithm \cite{shor1994algorithms} definitively proves that asymmetric cryptographic primitives relying on integer factorization and discrete logarithm problems can be broken in polynomial time. While the engineering timeline for a large-scale, fault-tolerant CRQC remains probabilistic, the global threat is already active due to the ``harvest now, decrypt later'' attack paradigm. In this model, sophisticated adversaries are currently intercepting and storing classical TLS traffic, awaiting quantum maturation to decrypt sensitive, long-term confidential data retroactively \cite{mosca2018cybersecurity}. 

In response to this existential cryptographic threat, the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) concluded its multi-year standardization effort on August 13, 2024, finalizing the first critical post-quantum cryptography (PQC) standards: FIPS 203 (ML-KEM), FIPS 204 (ML-DSA), and FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA) \cite{nist2024pqc}, effectively marking the starting gun for the global quantum migration.

The Internet of Things (IoT) represents arguably the most challenging and vulnerable deployment target in this post-quantum transition. By 2025, an estimated 75 billion devices will comprise the broader IoT ecosystem \cite{statista2021iot}, managing critical infrastructure across medical telemetry, industrial control systems, automotive V2X networking, and smart grids. Unlike cloud servers or mobile processors, IoT devices operate strictly within the severe resource parameters defined by RFC 7228 \cite{bormann2014coap}. Specifically, these constrained environments frequently possess minimal RAM available for cryptographic stacks ($<50$ KB), operate on microcontrollers executing under 50 MHz, and are bound by extreme multi-year battery lifecycles requiring sub-milliwatt power budgets.

The mathematical shift from classical cryptography to post-quantum lattices, hash trees, and codes introduces immense overhead, clashing with IoT constraints. Post-quantum public keys and signatures are frequently orders of magnitude larger than their ECC counterparts, ballooning TLS handshake network fragmentation, while the complex underlying matrix arithmetic saturates processor instruction cycles and memory stacks, accelerating energy depletion.

\subsection{Novelty and Research Gap}

While foundational literature exists profiling individual PQC algorithms on embedded hardware---such as the foundational work by the pqm4 project on Cortex-M4 \cite{kannwischer2019pqm4}, Fitzgibbon and Ottaviani's broad IoT benchmarking \cite{fitzgibbon2024benchmarking}, and real-world deployment assessments on ESP32 microcontrollers \cite{secrypt2025esp32}---a critical synthesis gap persists. Specifically, existing studies lack a unified cross-class systematic comparison mapping the entire spectrum of primary PQC candidates (\kyber{}, \dilithium{}, \falcon{}, \sphincs{}, and \ntru{}) identically against the exact tiered hardware definitions of RFC 7228 Class 0, Class 1, and Class 2 devices.

Furthermore, prior work heavily biases execution time metrics while frequently glossing over unified energy consumption models (combining both compute and radio transmission) and lacking deterministic, multi-criteria algorithm selection guides for practitioners.

This paper addresses these unmapped intersections by asking: \textit{Given the newest NIST-standardized algorithms, which schemes are viable on which specific IoT device classes, at what comparative cost against classical cryptography, and with what ultimate trade-offs?}

\subsection{Contributions}

To address these gaps, our contributions are structured as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item \textbf{Comprehensive Cross-Class Benchmarking:} We execute and analyze over 45,000 empirical measurements covering 14 algorithm parameter sets directly mapped against tightly constrained C0, C1, and C2 hardware simulations.
 \item \textbf{Expanded Energy \& Communication Analysis:} We construct a unified energy estimation model encompassing both microcontroller cycle drain and radio frequency (RF) transmission overheads for inflated PQC key sizes.
 \item \textbf{TOPSIS Decision Framework:} We employ a multi-attribute statistical decision matrix to objectively rank algorithms based on speed, memory footprint, energy, key size, and security scale, yielding the first deterministic PQC algorithm deployment guide tailored specifically for IoT system architects.
 \item \textbf{Classical vs. Quantum and Hybrid Profiling:} We establish classical bounds using RSA/ECC algorithms to quantify the ``migration cost,'' addressing overhead metrics for Hybrid TLS 1.3 models that currently bridge the classical-quantum divide.
\end{enumerate}
