\subsection{Latency and Execution Time Analysis}

Figure \ref{fig:keygen_time} outlines absolute execution times across primary key generation bounds. Across non-constrained parameters, classical baselines like ECDH-P256 complete full exchanges significantly under 2ms. During PQC migration, \kyber{}-512 dominates across all lattice KEM mechanisms, resolving comprehensively sub-10ms globally. Predictably, \falcon{} executes exceptionally fast verifications yet triggers significant latency barriers internally during generation algorithms owing firmly to complex Fourier bounds unsupported on Cortex-M0 microarchitectures without specialized Floating-Point Units (FPUs). 

The extreme execution time of \textbf{\sphincs{}} is caused almost entirely by its reliance on thousands of recursive SHA-3 hashing loops. While highly conservative security guarantees are maintained by the algorithm, the benchmarking charts for IoT C0 architectures are heavily skewed by its massive key generation delays.

\subsection{Communication Overhead Analysis}

Unlike classical ECDSA configurations transmitting a meager 64 bytes over RF interfaces, \dilithium{}-3 forces IoT communication busses to manage multiple multi-kilobyte transmissions across fragmented TCP packets.

\begin{itemize}
 \item \textbf{High Payload Cost:} SLH-DSA produces massive signatures between 17 KB and 49 KB. These large payloads fragment DTLS packets. This frequently causes the network to drop datagrams.
 \item \textbf{FN-DSA (FALCON) Efficiency:} \falcon{} generates highly compact 666-byte signatures. This low overhead easily beats competing algorithms. It shrinks radio latency significantly. However, it taxes the processor heavily during initialization.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{TLS 1.3 Handshake Overheads}

We mapped our results directly into the standard TLS 1.3 handshake flow \cite{campagna2020tls}. The overall latency increases sharply. The devices must process CertificateVerify messages packed with large lattice signatures. Sending these over wireless links takes time. On C1 endpoints, handling fragmented \dilithium{}-2 handshakes multiplies the total TLS execution time. We observed average latency increases of 15x to 25x.
